# 💱 What is RupeeShift? — A Plain English Guide

---

## 💡 The Big Idea

Every year, the value of the Indian Rupee against the US Dollar quietly shifts. Most years, it weakens — meaning one dollar buys more rupees than it did the year before.

This sounds like an abstract economic headline. But it has very real, very personal consequences for millions of Indians — whether you're a salaried employee, a freelancer, or running a business.

**RupeeShift is a tool that takes that invisible shift and puts a concrete number to it for your specific situation.**

> It answers the question: *"The dollar moved. What did that silently do to your money?"*

---

## 📉 Why Does This Matter?

Let's say one dollar bought **₹83** at the start of the financial year, and **₹87** by the end. That's about a 5% movement.

Depending on who you are, this same 5% either:

| Who you are | What the shift does |
|---|---|
| 🏢 Pay salaries in ₹, account in $ | 📈 Saved your company money in dollars |
| 💼 Earn in $, spend in ₹ | 📈 Boosted your rupee revenue |
| 💻 Invoice clients in $ | 📈 Gave you a quiet raise without negotiating |
| 👤 Rupee-paid at a dollar-earning company | 📉 Quietly cut your real value |

The problem is — nobody shows you this clearly. Your payslip looks the same. Your invoice looks the same. But in dollar terms, your situation has changed.

**RupeeShift makes that change visible.**

---

## 📊 The Four Stories RupeeShift Tells

### 🏢 Story 1: The Indian Subsidiary
*"Our parent company funds us in dollars. We pay our employees in rupees. Did the rupee weakening save us money?"*

**In plain English:** Imagine you manage the India office of a foreign company. You pay your team ₹1 crore a month. But your parent company in the US has to send you dollars to cover that. When the rupee weakens, they need to send *fewer* dollars to cover the same rupee amount.

RupeeShift calculates exactly how many dollars your parent company saved (or spent extra) month by month — and tells you what hike you can give next year while still keeping the dollar cost the same or lower.

**Real example:** At ₹83/$, ₹1 Cr/month costs $120,481. At ₹87/$, the same payroll costs only $114,943 — a saving of **$5,538/month** with no change in employee salaries.

---

### 💼 Story 2: The IT Exporter
*"We earn in dollars, we spend in rupees. Did the currency shift help our margins?"*

**In plain English:** Say your IT company has a $1 million annual contract with a US client. When the rupee weakens, that same $1 million converts to *more* rupees. Your revenue in rupees goes up — even though your client paid the exact same amount.

RupeeShift shows you how much extra INR you made purely because of the exchange rate, how that affects your ability to give salary hikes, and what a contract renewal at a lower dollar rate really means in rupee terms.

**Real example:** $1M at ₹83/$ = ₹8.3 Cr. The same $1M at ₹87/$ = ₹8.7 Cr — that's **₹40 lakh extra revenue** at zero additional effort.

---

### 💻 Story 3: The Freelancer
*"I invoice clients in dollars. Did the rupee weakening give me a raise I didn't ask for?"*

**In plain English:** You charge a client $2,000 a month. When the rupee weakens from ₹83/$ to ₹87/$, your monthly income jumps from ₹1,66,000 to ₹1,74,000 — without changing your rate.

That's a silent raise of **₹8,000/month** or **₹96,000/year** — just from the exchange rate. But when you negotiate your next rate card, you need to think carefully — if the dollar rate shifts back, that raise disappears.

RupeeShift shows you exactly how much extra you've been earning, and what different renewal rates mean for your rupee income next year.

---

### 👤 Story 4: The INR Employee
*"My company earns in dollars. I get paid in rupees. Am I falling behind?"*

**In plain English:** You work at a company that earns most of its revenue in dollars. Your salary is ₹12 lakh per year. When the rupee weakens, your employer's dollar revenue converts to more rupees — but your salary stays the same.

In dollar terms, you're now cheaper to employ than you were last year. Your real value has quietly eroded. The 10% hike you got might not even bring you back to where you were in dollar terms.

**Real example:** ₹12 LPA ÷ ₹83/$ = $14,458/year. At ₹87/$, the same ₹12 LPA = only $13,793/year. That's a **$665/year erosion** — even before any salary negotiation.

RupeeShift calculates exactly how much your salary has eroded in USD terms, and tells you the minimum hike percentage you need just to break even.

## 🚀 How to Use RupeeShift

1. **Pick your financial year** — FY 2021–22 through FY 2025–26, with live ECB exchange rate data

2. **Choose your scenario** — Subsidiary, IT Exporter, Freelancer, or INR Employee

3. **Enter your numbers** — salary, revenue, or contract value using the sliders

4. **Read the insight** — a plain-English sentence at the top immediately tells you the bottom line

5. **Explore the charts** — month-by-month breakdown shows exactly *when* the shift happened

6. **Use the increment table** — see break-even percentages for planning hikes or contract renewals

7. **Export if needed** — download the full analysis as Excel or CSV for presentations or negotiations

## ⚖️ Understanding the Break-Even Number

One of the most important outputs in RupeeShift is the **break-even percentage**.

Think of it this way:

> If the rupee weakened by **5%** this year...
> - A **5% salary hike** gets you exactly back to where you were in dollar terms — no better, no worse
> - Anything **below 5%** is effectively a pay cut in real terms
> - Anything **above 5%** is a genuine raise

```
Break-Even % = ((FY End Rate − FY Start Rate) / FY Start Rate) × 100
```

RupeeShift highlights this break-even number clearly for every scenario, so you can have an informed, data-backed conversation with your employer or client.

## ⚡ Quick Facts

- The Indian Rupee has weakened by more than **₹10 per dollar** since FY 2021–22 (from ~₹74 to ~₹85)
- That represents roughly a **12% cumulative depreciation** — a meaningful number for anyone with a USD link
- **No data you enter is ever sent to a server** — all calculations happen entirely in your browser

---

## 🚫 What RupeeShift Is NOT

- ❌ It is **not** investment advice
- ❌ It is **not** a currency prediction tool — it only analyses historical data for completed financial years
- ❌ It is **not** a tax or compliance calculator
- ❌ It does **not** account for hedging, TDS, remittance fees, or transaction spreads
- ❌ It does **not** store any of your data

---

## 🌐 Where Does the Data Come From?

RupeeShift fetches USD/INR exchange rates from the **[Frankfurter API](https://www.frankfurter.app)**, which publishes official daily rates from the **European Central Bank (ECB)**. These are mid-market reference rates — the same ones used as the global benchmark.

Monthly averages are the arithmetic mean of all daily rates within each calendar month.

Rates are cached in your browser session after the first load, so the app works even if you go offline.
